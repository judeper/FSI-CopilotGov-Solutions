## Findings

### F1 — Document budgets as alerts, not spending caps

- **Severity:** Major
- **Location:** lines 13 and 37
- **Current:** "Beginning April 2026... cost cap monitoring through Azure Cost Management... Does not configure PAYG billing policies or spending caps..."
- **Issue:** Microsoft 365 Copilot pay-as-you-go budgets trigger notifications but do not enforce a cap or prevent spending beyond the budget. Calling them spending caps can mislead administrators.
- **Suggested change:** "Replace cost cap/spending cap language with budget alert thresholds, and state that budgets do not enforce a hard cap."
- **Source:** https://learn.microsoft.com/microsoft-365/copilot/pay-as-you-go/setup (last updated 2025-11-18)
- **Quote:** "the system doesn't enforce the budget or prevent your organization from exceeding it."

### F2 — Include least-privileged license inventory permission

- **Severity:** Minor
- **Location:** line 119
- **Current:** "Microsoft Graph application permissions: `Reports.Read.All`, `Directory.Read.All`, `User.Read.All`."
- **Issue:** `Directory.Read.All` can read subscribedSkus, but Microsoft Graph lists `LicenseAssignment.Read.All` as the least-privileged permission for `/subscribedSkus`.
