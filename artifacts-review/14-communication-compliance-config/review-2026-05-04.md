### `solutions\14-communication-compliance-config\README.md`

#### Finding F1 — Update Communication Compliance license naming

- **Severity:** Major
- **Location:** line 85
- **Current text:** "Microsoft Purview Communication Compliance licensing such as Microsoft 365 E5 Compliance or an equivalent add-on."
- **Issue:** Microsoft Learn now describes the current licensing as Microsoft Purview Suite, with Microsoft 365 E5 Compliance identified as the former name. Keeping the former SKU name as the primary label can mislead tenant validation.
- **Recommended correction:** "Microsoft Purview Suite (formerly Microsoft 365 E5 Compliance), Office 365 E5, or Office 365 E3 with the Advanced Compliance add-on, as applicable to covered users."
- **Source URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/purview/communication-compliance-plan
- **Source last updated:** 2025-08-12
- **Quote from source:** "Microsoft Purview Suite (formerly known as Microsoft 365 E5 Compliance) license"

#### Finding F2 — Replace non-current Communication Compliance role names

- **Severity:** Major
- **Location:** line 86
- **Current text:** "Reviewer and administrator roles for Microsoft Purview Communication Compliance, Compliance Administrator, and read-only auditors."
- **Issue:** The named role is not the current Communication Compliance role-group name. Microsoft Learn uses role groups such as `Communication Compliance Admins`, `Communication Compliance Analysts`, `Communication Compliance Investigators`, and `Communication Compliance Viewers`.
- **Recommended correction:** "Administrator access through the `Communication Compliance Admins` role group or Compliance Administrator, reviewer access through `Communication Compliance Analysts` or `Communication Compliance Investigators`, and read-only access through `Communication Compliance Viewers` where appropriate."
- **Source URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/purview/communication-compliance-permissions
- **Source last updated:** 2025-08-14
- **Quote from source:** "Communication Compliance Admins Communication Compliance Analysts Communication Compliance Investigators Communication Compliance Viewers"

#### Finding F1 — Risky AI usage indicator examples do not match Learn terminology

- **Severity:** Major
- **Location:** lines 142-144
- **Current text:** "IRM policy templates reference built-in indicators for sensitive prompt content, unusual prompt volume, and AI-generated response patterns..."

- **Current text:** "Copilot video recap for Teams meetings (March 2026) creates AI-generated video highlights from recorded meetings..."

- **Quote from source:** "After the meeting or event ends, your users can access the following AI powered features"

#### Finding F3 — Retention claim for recap artifacts is over-specific

- **Severity:** Major
- **Location:** lines 156-157
- **Current text:** "Video recap artifacts are subject to the same record retention requirements as the source meeting recordings and transcripts... separate retention policies for video recaps are not required when source artifact retention is already configured."
- **Issue:** Microsoft Learn documents retention locations for Teams recordings, transcripts, Teams messages, Copilot experiences, and AI apps; it does not support the blanket statement that recap artifacts inherit source recording/transcript retention or never need separate retention planning.
- **Recommended correction:** State that Teams recordings and transcripts require retention coverage through the documented OneDrive, SharePoint, Microsoft 365 group, Teams, and Copilot/AI-app locations, and separately validate Intelligent recap/audio recap artifacts against current Purview retention behavior.
- **Source URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/purview/create-retention-policies
- **Source last updated:** 2026-02-26
- **Quote from source:** "To retain or delete Teams meeting recordings with their transcripts from user chats, you'll need a retention policy"
