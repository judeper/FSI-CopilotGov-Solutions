### `solutions/05-dlp-policy-governance/README.md`

#### Finding F1 — Prompt-SIT blocking needs preview disclosure and current location name

- **Severity:** Major
- **Location:** line 13
- **Current text:** "Microsoft Purview DLP now supports **Microsoft 365 Copilot** as a dedicated policy location. DLP policies can help block Copilot from processing prompts containing sensitive information types..."
- **Issue:** The current Microsoft Learn article names the location **Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Chat**. It also distinguishes multiple Copilot DLP capabilities: restricting external web search for sensitive prompts is preview, blocking sensitive information types in prompts is preview, and blocking sensitivity-labeled files/emails from Copilot processing is generally available. The README presents prompt-SIT blocking as a current GA-style capability without the preview qualifier.
- **Recommended correction:** Rename the note to **Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Chat policy location** and state that sensitive-information-type prompt blocking is in preview, while sensitivity-label protection for files and emails is generally available.
- **Source URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/purview/dlp-microsoft365-copilot-location-learn-about
- **Source last updated:** 2026-04-22 (updated_at 2026-04-29)
- **Quote from source:** "This feature is in preview."

#### Finding F2 — Copilot DLP scope is modeled too broadly as responses and per-workload coverage

- **Severity:** Major
- **Location:** lines 9, 23, 54-64, 111, 123
- **Current text:** "...policy settings that target Copilot prompts, Copilot responses, and grounded content access patterns..." and "Teams chat and channel prompts or responses... SharePoint... OneDrive... Exchange content paths..."
- **Issue:** The current Copilot DLP article documents a **Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Chat** policy location with specific supported conditions/actions. It is not configured as one policy spanning Teams, SharePoint, OneDrive, and Exchange locations; selecting the Copilot location disables other policy locations. File/email coverage is also limited to supported files in SharePoint Online/OneDrive for Business and emails sent on or after January 1, 2025, with calendar invites unsupported. The README should distinguish the Copilot policy location from separate workload DLP policies.
- **Recommended correction:** Replace the per-workload response wording with current supported capabilities: prompt text SIT controls, external web-search grounding restriction (preview), and sensitivity-label controls for supported files/emails used in response summarization. Document separate workload DLP policies as complementary controls, not as the Copilot policy location itself.
- **Source URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/purview/dlp-microsoft365-copilot-location-learn-about
- **Source last updated:** 2026-04-22 (updated_at 2026-04-29)
- **Quote from source:** "When you select the **Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Chat** policy location, all other locations for that policy are disabled."

#### Finding F3 — Prompt file-upload caveat is missing from scope boundaries or limitations

- **Severity:** Major
- **Location:** lines 13, 133-138
- **Current text:** "...supporting prompt-level data loss prevention for AI-assisted interactions." and known limitations do not mention uploaded prompt files.
- **Issue:** Microsoft Learn states that DLP Copilot checks the text typed into a prompt but cannot scan contents of files uploaded directly into prompts. Omitting this caveat may lead operators to overestimate prompt-level DLP coverage.
- **Recommended correction:** Add a Scope Boundaries or Known limitations bullet that DLP for Copilot does not scan contents of files uploaded directly into prompts; it evaluates the prompt text itself.
- **Source URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/purview/dlp-microsoft365-copilot-location-learn-about
- **Source last updated:** 2026-04-22 (updated_at 2026-04-29)
- **Quote from source:** "DLP can't scan the contents of files that you upload directly into prompts"

#### Finding F4 — Copilot DLP role guidance omits current AI-specific Purview roles

- **Severity:** Major
- **Location:** lines 47, 77-78
- **Current text:** "Operators have Compliance Administrator, Security Reader, or DLP Compliance Management permissions" and exception routing uses "Compliance Administrator" / "senior compliance sign-off and CCO approval".
- **Issue:** The current Copilot DLP article lists AI-specific roles and Purview-prefixed role groups for creating or editing DLP policies that safeguard Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Chat. The README omits Entra AI Admin, Purview Data Security AI Admin, and Purview Data Security AI Admins, and it presents Security Reader as sufficient operator permission without distinguishing read-only review from Copilot DLP policy editing.
- **Recommended correction:** Update prerequisites to distinguish read-only reviewers from policy editors. For policy create/edit, include the current Copilot DLP role list from Microsoft Learn and remove or qualify Security Reader as read-only only.
- **Source URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/purview/dlp-microsoft365-copilot-location-learn-about
- **Source last updated:** 2026-04-22 (updated_at 2026-04-29)
- **Quote from source:** "Purview Data Security AI Admin - Role for editing Data Loss Prevention policies related to Copilot"

### `solutions/05-dlp-policy-governance/config/default-config.json`

#### Finding F5 — `copilotSignals` uses non-canonical Copilot DLP signal names

- **Severity:** Major
- **Location:** lines 47-57
- **Current text:** "`copilotWorkloads`: Teams, SharePoint, OneDrive, Exchange" and "`copilotSignals`: CopilotPrompt, CopilotResponse, GroundedContentAccess"
- **Issue:** Microsoft Learn documents the Copilot policy location and supported conditions/actions, not DLP signal names called `CopilotResponse` or `GroundedContentAccess`. The location also disables other policy locations, so using Teams/SharePoint/OneDrive/Exchange as the Copilot policy-location model can mislead implementers.
- **Recommended correction:** Replace `copilotSignals` with canonical capability identifiers aligned to Learn, such as `sensitiveInformationTypesInPrompts`, `externalWebSearchGroundingRestriction`, and `sensitivityLabelsOnSupportedFilesAndEmails`, each with preview/GA metadata. If workload DLP policies are tracked, store them separately from the Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Chat policy location.
- **Source URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/purview/dlp-microsoft365-copilot-location-learn-about
- **Source last updated:** 2026-04-22 (updated_at 2026-04-29)
- **Quote from source:** "The **Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Chat** policy location supports the following conditions and actions"

### `solutions/05-dlp-policy-governance/docs/architecture.md`

#### Finding F6 — Architecture conflates Copilot policy location with workload DLP locations

- **Severity:** Major
- **Location:** lines 15, 20-24, 38-40
- **Current text:** "Microsoft Purview DLP and Security and Compliance PowerShell... Supplies DLP policy metadata for Copilot workloads" and the data flow starts from "Purview DLP and Graph policy state" into a Copilot baseline.
- **Issue:** The architecture describes Copilot-scoped DLP as if it is organized by workload coverage. Current Microsoft Learn says the Copilot policy location is **Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Chat** and disables all other policy locations in that policy. This should be represented separately from Exchange, SharePoint, OneDrive, Teams, and endpoint DLP policy metadata.
- **Recommended correction:** Update the architecture to show two layers: (1) Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Chat location policies for supported Copilot interactions, and (2) complementary workload DLP policies for Exchange, SharePoint, OneDrive, Teams, devices, and endpoint channels.
- **Source URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/purview/dlp-microsoft365-copilot-location-learn-about
- **Source last updated:** 2026-04-22 (updated_at 2026-04-29)
- **Quote from source:** "When you select the **Microsoft 365 Copilot and Copilot Chat** policy location, all other locations for that policy are disabled."
